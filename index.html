With a single electronic card, LOUISE simplifies the way cash is distributed through various programmes.







The goal of LOUISE is to address different vulnerabilities of nearly one million Syrian refugees and disadvantaged Lebanese in a simple, cost effective and mutually reinforcing way. This is a first, allowing refugees to give back to hosting communities by boosting the economy. The Common Card provides dignity and enhanced financial independence, while addressing protection, education and food security challenges

 
”

Because aid is never sufficient to cover all needs, we are constantly trying to do better - in the most cost-efficient way – in order to reach the maximum number of people.

LOUISE stands for Lebanon’s One Unified Inter-Organizational System for E-cards; a system consisting of one common vulnerability assessment, one financial service provider, one distribution of e-cards, one monitoring and evaluation system, one training for beneficiaries, one communication system, one call-centre for e-card queries and one information portal.

LOUISE is a joint effort of UNHCR, WFP, UNICEF and LCC (Lebanon Cash Consortium) to ensure an efficient, transparent and cost effective way of delivering humanitarian aid. To date, 167,000 households have a common card.
